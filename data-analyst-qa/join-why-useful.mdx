## 💻 La Respuesta Técnica

Los **JOINs** permiten combinar información de tablas relacionadas, lo cual es esencial porque:

1. **Normalización**: Los datos se almacenan en tablas separadas para evitar redundancia
2. **Contexto de negocio**: Unir transacciones con dimensiones (clientes, productos, regiones)
3. **Análisis enriquecido**: Responder preguntas que requieren múltiples fuentes de datos

**Sin JOINs**, necesitarías tablas denormalizadas o múltiples queries manuales.

**Ejemplo práctico:**
```sql
-- Pregunta: ¿Cuánto vendió cada cliente?
-- Datos en tablas separadas:
-- customers (id, name, email)
-- orders (id, customer_id, amount, date)

SELECT c.name, SUM(o.amount) as total_spent
FROM customers c
JOIN orders o ON c.id = o.customer_id
GROUP BY c.name;
```

## 🇺🇸 El Script en Inglés (Para la entrevista)

> *"JOINs are the backbone of relational data analysis. In a normalized database—which is the standard design for transactional systems—data is intentionally split across tables to reduce redundancy and maintain integrity. Customers live in one table, orders in another, products in yet another. JOINs let me reconstruct the complete picture by connecting these related entities. Without them, I'd either need denormalized tables, which introduce data quality risks, or I'd have to run separate queries and manually stitch results together, which is error-prone and inefficient. In my day-to-day work as an analyst, almost every meaningful question spans multiple tables: revenue by customer segment requires joining orders with customer demographics; product performance needs orders joined with product categories. JOINs make that possible in a single, efficient query."*

## 🔑 Vocabulario Clave

* **Relational data analysis:** Análisis de datos relacionales
* **Normalized database:** Base de datos normalizada
* **Redundancy:** Redundancia
* **Denormalized tables:** Tablas denormalizadas
* **Error-prone:** Propenso a errores
