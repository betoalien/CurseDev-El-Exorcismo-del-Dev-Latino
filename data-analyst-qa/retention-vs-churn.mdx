## 🛑 El Error del Junior

"Uno es quedarse y el otro es irse"—correcto pero incompleto. No explica cómo se miden ni por qué son dos caras de la misma moneda.

## 🧠 El Enfoque Curse Dev (Contra-pregunta)

* **Tú:** *"They're two sides of the same coin, but the way you measure each can vary. Would you like me to walk through the different calculation methods?"*

## 💻 La Respuesta Técnica

**Retención** y **churn** miden el mismo fenómeno desde perspectivas opuestas:

- **Retención**: Porcentaje de usuarios que **permanecen activos** en un periodo
- **Churn**: Porcentaje de usuarios que **abandonan** en un periodo

**Relación:** `Retención + Churn = 100%` (para la misma cohorte y periodo)

**Fórmulas comunes:**
```
Retención = (Usuarios activos al final - Nuevos usuarios) / Usuarios al inicio × 100
Churn = Usuarios perdidos / Usuarios al inicio × 100
```

**Tipos de churn:**
- **Voluntario**: El usuario decide cancelar
- **Involuntario**: Pago fallido, problemas técnicos

## 🇺🇸 El Script en Inglés (Para la entrevista)

> *"Retention and churn are inverse metrics that both measure customer continuity, just from opposite angles. Retention tells you what percentage of customers stayed active over a given period—it's your ability to keep customers engaged and deriving value. Churn tells you what percentage left—it's your leakage rate. Mathematically, if 80% of your customers remain active this month, your retention is 80% and your churn is 20%. But here's where it gets nuanced: how you define 'active' and 'left' matters enormously. For a subscription business, churn is clear—a cancelled subscription. For a free product, you need to define inactivity thresholds, like 'no login in 30 days.' I also distinguish between voluntary churn (the customer chose to leave) and involuntary churn (payment failed, technical issues). The latter is often fixable with better operations. In my analysis, I track both retention and churn by cohort—customers who joined in the same period—because that reveals whether product improvements are actually extending customer lifetime. A rising retention rate or falling churn rate over cohorts is one of the strongest signals of product-market fit."*

## 🔑 Vocabulario Clave

* **Inverse metrics:** Métricas inversas
* **Customer continuity:** Continuidad del cliente
* **Leakage rate:** Tasa de fuga
* **Inactivity thresholds:** Umbrales de inactividad
* **Product-market fit:** Ajuste producto-mercado
