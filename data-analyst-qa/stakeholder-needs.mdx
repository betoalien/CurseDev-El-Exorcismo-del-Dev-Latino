## 🛑 El Error del Junior

"Para que le guste"—superficial. No entiende que dashboards sin utilidad real son desperdicio.

## 🧠 El Enfoque Curse Dev (Contra-pregunta)

* **Tú:** *"Stakeholder discovery is the most critical phase of dashboard development. Would it help if I walked through my requirements-gathering process?"*

## 💻 La Respuesta Técnica

**Entender al stakeholder evita:**

1. **Dashboards inútiles**: Datos correctos, pero que no responden sus preguntas reales
2. **Retrabajo**: Reconstruir el dashboard 3 veces hasta "atinarle"
3. **Adopción baja**: Stakeholder no lo usa porque no le resuelve

**Proceso de descubrimiento:**

**1. Identificar decisiones:**
- "¿Qué decisiones necesitas tomar con este dashboard?"
- "¿Qué harías diferente si ves X vs Y?"

**2. Entender contexto:**
- Nivel técnico: ¿SQL o business terms?
- Frecuencia: ¿Diario, semanal, mensual?
- Urgencia: ¿Necesita actuar rápido o solo revisar?

**3. Validar KPIs:**
- "¿Cómo defines 'cliente activo'?"
- "¿Qué target consideran éxito?"

**4. Prototipar temprano:**
- Mockup en papel o Figma antes de construir
- Validar: "¿Esto te ayuda a tomar decisiones?"

## 🇺🇸 El Script en Inglés (Para la entrevista)

> *"Understanding the stakeholder is the difference between a dashboard that gets used and one that becomes shelf-ware. I've learned this the hard way—building what I thought was perfect, only to find out it didn't answer the questions they actually care about. My discovery process starts with decisions: 'What decisions will you make with this dashboard?' If they can't articulate that, we don't have clarity yet. Then I dig into their context: Are they technical, preferring raw numbers and filters? Or do they need high-level summaries with plain-English labels? How often will they check this—daily operational monitoring or monthly strategic reviews? What actions might they take based on what they see? I also validate definitions upfront: 'When you say active customer, what exactly counts?' I've seen deals break because marketing and sales defined 'lead' differently. Finally, I prototype early—a quick mockup in Figma or even on paper—and ask, 'If you saw this every morning, would it help you do your job?' That feedback loop saves weeks of rework. The best dashboards I've built came from deep stakeholder discovery, not from assuming I knew what they needed."*

## 🔑 Vocabulario Clave

* **Shelf-ware:** Software que se queda en la estantería (no se usa)
* **Discovery process:** Proceso de descubrimiento
* **Requirements-gathering:** Recolección de requisitos
* **Mockup:** Maqueta / prototipo
* **Feedback loop:** Ciclo de retroalimentación
