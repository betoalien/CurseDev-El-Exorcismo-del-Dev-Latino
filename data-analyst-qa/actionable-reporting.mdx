## 🛑 El Error del Junior

"Que se puede actuar"—tautología. No explica qué hace que un reporte impulse acción real.

## 🧠 El Enfoque Curse Dev (Contra-pregunta)

* **Tú:** *"Actionable reporting is a mindset shift. Would it help if I explained how I transform 'here's what happened' into 'here's what to do'?"*

## 💻 La Respuesta Técnica

Un reporte **accionable** no solo muestra datos—**facilita entender qué hacer después**:

**Características de reportes accionables:**

| No Accionable | Accionable |
|--------------|------------|
| "Ventas: $1M" | "Ventas: $1M (-15% vs target). Segmento X requiere atención" |
| Tabla con 50 filas | Top 5 oportunidades + bottom 5 riesgos |
| Solo datos históricos | Datos + recomendación basada en patrones |
| ¿Qué pasó? | ¿Qué pasó? ¿Por qué? ¿Qué hacer? |

**Elementos clave:**

1. **Contexto**: Comparativos vs target, periodo anterior, benchmark
2. **Insight explícito**: "El drop se concentra en mobile LATAM"
3. **Recomendación**: "Sugiero investigar checkout flow en Android"
4. **Owner claro**: Cada métrica tiene responsable identificado
5. **Urgencia**: Semáforo o priorización que guía atención

## 🇺🇸 El Script en Inglés (Para la entrevista)

> *"An actionable report answers three questions: What happened? Why does it matter? What should we do about it? Most reports stop at question one—they're autopsies that describe the past. Actionable reports drive decisions. Here's how I build them. First, context: a number without comparison is meaningless. I always include targets, prior period comparisons, or benchmarks so stakeholders know if they're looking at good news or a problem. Second, explicit insights: I don't make them hunt for the story. I'll write, 'Conversion dropped 20%, driven entirely by mobile users in Latin America.' That points them where to look. Third, recommendations: based on patterns I've seen, I'll suggest next steps—'I recommend investigating the Android checkout flow; we've seen similar issues after the last deployment.' Fourth, clear ownership: every metric should have a person accountable. If no one owns it, no one will act on it. Finally, prioritization: I use visual cues like red/yellow/green indicators to signal urgency. The goal is that a stakeholder can read the report and immediately know: Are we on track? Where's the fire? What should I do today? If they finish reading and think 'interesting, but now what?'—it's not actionable."*

## 🔑 Vocabulario Clave

* **Mindset shift:** Cambio de mentalidad
* **Autopsies:** Autopsias (reportes post-mortem)
* **Explicit insights:** Insights explícitos
* **Ownership:** Responsabilidad / propiedad
* **Prioritization:** Priorización
