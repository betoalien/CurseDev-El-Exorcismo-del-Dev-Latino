## 🛑 El Error del Junior

Da una fórmula matemática sin contexto. No explica qué cuenta como "conversión" ni por qué esta métrica importa para el negocio.

## 🧠 El Enfoque Curse Dev (Contra-pregunta)

* **Tú:** *"Conversion rate is one of those metrics that sounds simple but has important nuances. Would it help if I explained it with a concrete funnel example?"*

## 💻 La Respuesta Técnica

La **tasa de conversión** mide el porcentaje de usuarios que completan una **acción deseada** dentro de un funnel:

**Fórmula:**
```
Tasa de Conversión = (Conversiones / Total de usuarios o sesiones) × 100
```

**Puntos clave:**
1. **Definir conversión**: ¿Qué acción importa? (compra, registro, descarga, clic)
2. **Denominador correcto**: Usuarios expuestos a la oportunidad de convertir
3. **Segmentar**: La tasa general oculta variaciones por canal, dispositivo, región

**Ejemplo:**
- 10,000 visitantes al sitio
- 250 compras completadas
- Tasa de conversión = 250 / 10,000 = **2.5%**

## 🇺🇸 El Script en Inglés (Para la entrevista)

> *"Conversion rate is fundamentally a measure of effectiveness—how well you're turning opportunities into outcomes. At its core, it's a simple ratio: the number of users who complete a desired action divided by the total number who had the opportunity to do so, expressed as a percentage. But the devil is in the details. First, you must clearly define what a 'conversion' is—for an e-commerce site, it's purchases; for a SaaS product, it might be free-to-paid upgrades; for a media site, it could be newsletter signups. Second, you need the right denominator: only count users who actually had a chance to convert. And third—and this is critical—conversion rates almost always vary by segment. A 3% overall rate might hide a 10% rate on desktop and a 1% rate on mobile, which points to a very different problem. In my analysis work, I always break down conversion rates by traffic source, device, geography, and user cohort. That's where you find the real insights—like discovering that paid search converts at 5% but social media at 1%, which tells you where to double down or where there's friction to fix."*

## 🔑 Vocabulario Clave

* **Measure of effectiveness:** Medida de efectividad
* **Desired action:** Acción deseada
* **Free-to-paid upgrades:** Actualizaciones de gratis a pago
* **Denominator:** Denominador
* **Traffic source:** Fuente de tráfico
