## 🛑 El Error del Junior

"Se duplican las filas"—correcto pero incompleto. No explica cómo esto afecta agregaciones y métricas de negocio.

## 🧠 El Enfoque Curse Dev (Contra-pregunta)

* **Tú:** *"One-to-many relationships have a direct impact on query results, especially aggregations. Would you like me to show how this can lead to incorrect metrics if not handled properly?"*

## 💻 La Respuesta Técnica

En una relación **uno a muchos**, una fila de la tabla principal coincide con múltiples filas de la tabla secundaria, lo que **multiplica los resultados**:

**Impactos clave:**
1. **Filas duplicadas**: La tabla izquierda aparece múltiples veces
2. **Agregaciones infladas**: COUNT, SUM pueden dar resultados incorrectos
3. **Métricas distorsionadas**: Promedios, tasas, conversiones se calculan mal

**Ejemplo práctico:**
```sql
-- Un cliente tiene 5 pedidos → 5 filas en el JOIN
SELECT c.name, c.email, o.order_id
FROM customers c
JOIN orders o ON c.id = o.customer_id;
-- Resultado: 5 filas para el mismo cliente

-- ERROR COMÚN: Contar clientes después del JOIN
SELECT COUNT(c.id) as customer_count  -- ¡Devuelve 5, no 1!
FROM customers c JOIN orders o ON c.id = o.customer_id;

-- SOLUCIÓN: COUNT DISTINCT o agregar antes de hacer JOIN
SELECT COUNT(DISTINCT c.id) as customer_count  -- Correcto: 1
FROM customers c JOIN orders o ON c.id = o.customer_id;
```

## 🇺🇸 El Script en Inglés (Para la entrevista)

> *"One-to-many relationships are fundamental to relational databases, but they introduce a critical pitfall that I've seen trip up many analysts: row multiplication. When you join a 'one' table to a 'many' table—like customers to orders—each customer row gets repeated for every matching order. If a customer has five orders, you get five rows for that customer in the result. This is correct behavior for detail queries, but it breaks aggregations if you're not careful. A COUNT of customers becomes a COUNT of customer-order combinations. A SUM of customer attributes gets multiplied. My approach is to always be aware of the cardinality of my joins. If I'm joining to a 'many' side, I either use COUNT(DISTINCT ...) to avoid double-counting, or I aggregate the 'many' table first in a subquery or CTE before joining. This ensures my metrics reflect the true cardinality of the business entities I'm measuring."*

## 🔑 Vocabulario Clave

* **Row multiplication:** Multiplicación de filas
* **Pitfall:** Trampa / error común
* **Cardinality:** Cardinalidad
* **Double-counting:** Conteo doble
* **Business entities:** Entidades de negocio
