## 🛑 El Error del Junior

"Son métricas que no importan"—demasiado absoluto. No explica cuándo una métrica SÍ es accionable ni da ejemplos concretos.

## 🧠 El Enfoque Curse Dev (Contra-pregunta)

* **Tú:** *"Vanity metrics are seductive because they look good on paper. Would it help if I explained how to distinguish them from metrics that actually drive decisions?"*

## 💻 La Respuesta Técnica

Las **vanity metrics** son métricas que **se ven bien** pero no reflejan valor real de negocio ni guían decisiones accionables:

**Características de vanity metrics:**
1. **Fáciles de inflar**: Crecen sin impacto real en el negocio
2. **No accionables**: No sabes qué hacer cuando cambian
3. **Sin contexto**: Un número grande no significa éxito
4. **Distraen**: Enfocarse en ellas desvía atención de lo que importa

**Ejemplos comunes:**
- Visitas totales al sitio (sin conversión)
- Likes en redes sociales (sin engagement real)
- Descargas de app (sin usuarios activos)
- Total de usuarios registrados (sin actividad)

**Alternativas accionables:**
- Tasa de conversión, no visitas
- Engagement rate, no likes
- DAU/MAU, no descargas
- Usuarios activos, no registros

## 🇺🇸 El Script en Inglés (Para la entrevista)

> *"Vanity metrics are what I call 'feel-good numbers'—they look impressive on a dashboard or in a pitch deck, but they don't actually inform decision-making or reflect genuine business health. The classic examples are total website visits, social media followers, or app downloads. These can grow indefinitely without translating to revenue, retention, or any outcome that keeps the lights on. The danger isn't just that they're meaningless—it's that they're seductive. A founder can raise funding showing user growth, but if those users aren't active or monetizing, the business will fail. As an analyst, my job is to steer stakeholders toward actionable metrics—ones that tie directly to business outcomes and tell you what to do next. Instead of 'total signups,' I report 'activated users'—people who completed a meaningful action. Instead of 'page views,' I track 'conversion rate by traffic source.' The test I use is simple: if this metric doubles tomorrow, does that mean we're succeeding? And if it drops by half, do we know what action to take? If the answer to either is 'not really,' it's probably a vanity metric."*

## 🔑 Vocabulario Clave

* **Feel-good numbers:** Números que hacen sentir bien
* **Pitch deck:** Presentación para inversores
* **Genuine business health:** Salud real del negocio
* **Actionable metrics:** Métricas accionables
* **Activated users:** Usuarios activados
