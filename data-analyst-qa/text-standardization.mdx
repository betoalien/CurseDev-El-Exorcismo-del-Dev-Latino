## 🛑 El Error del Junior

"Para que se vea bonito"—superficial. No entiende que la estandarización afecta directamente la precisión del análisis.

## 💻 La Respuesta Técnica

La **estandarización de texto** es crítica para la **integridad analítica**:

**Problemas sin estandarizar:**

| Columna | Variaciones | Impacto |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| Ciudad | "Monterrey", "monterrey", "MTY", "Monterrey " | 4 grupos separados en GROUP BY |
| País | "USA", "U.S.A.", "United States", "Estados Unidos" | JOINs fallan, métricas fragmentadas |
| Categoría | "Electronics", "electronics ", "ELECTRONICS" | Reportes de ventas incorrectos |

**Consecuencias:**
1. **Agregaciones incorrectas**: Ventas de "México" y "Mexico" no se suman
2. **JOINs incompletos**: "CA" no hace match con "California"
3. **Filtros que excluyen**: WHERE country = 'Germany' pierde "DEUTSCHLAND"
4. **Dashboards fragmentados**: Mismo país aparece como 5 líneas diferentes

**Técnicas de estandarización:**
```sql
-- Normalización básica
SELECT
    TRIM(UPPER(city)) as city_clean
FROM raw_data;

-- Mapeo de valores
SELECT
    CASE
        WHEN country IN ('USA', 'US', 'United States', 'EE.UU.') THEN 'United States'
        WHEN country IN ('MX', 'México', 'Mexico') THEN 'Mexico'
        ELSE country
    END as country_standard
FROM raw_data;
```

## 🇺🇸 El Script en Inglés (Para la entrevista)

> *"Text standardization is one of those unglamorous tasks that separates reliable analytics from broken reports. The issue is that SQL is case-sensitive and character-exact in most databases—'Mexico' and 'México' and 'MX' are completely different strings. When you GROUP BY a non-standardized column, you get separate buckets for each variation, fragmenting your metrics. When you JOIN on inconsistent values, rows silently fail to match, giving you incomplete results. I learned this the hard way when a country-level revenue report showed 'United States' at $5M and 'USA' at $2M—the same country, split by data entry variation. Now, I build standardization into my data pipeline. I create cleaned columns with consistent casing (usually UPPER or INITCAP), trimmed whitespace, and mapped synonyms for known variations. For countries, I use ISO codes as the canonical standard. For categories, I maintain a lookup table that maps all variations to canonical values. This upfront work pays dividends: my GROUP BYs produce accurate aggregations, my JOINs don't lose rows, and my dashboards don't show five variations of the same category. It's data hygiene, and it's non-negotiable for trustworthy analysis."*

## 🔑 Vocabulario Clave

* **Case-sensitive:** Sensible a mayúsculas/minúsculas
* **Character-exact:** Exacto por carácter
* **Fragmenting metrics:** Fragmentar métricas
* **Data hygiene:** Higiene de datos
* **Non-negotiable:** No negociable
