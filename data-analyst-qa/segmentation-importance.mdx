## 🛑 El Error del Junior

"Para ver diferencias"—vago. No menciona la paradoja de Simpson ni cómo los promedios generales engañan.

## 🧠 El Enfoque Curse Dev (Contra-pregunta)

* **Tú:** *"Segmentation is where the real insights hide. Would it help if I showed an example where the aggregate told one story but the segments told another?"*

## 💻 La Respuesta Técnica

La **segmentación** divide datos en **grupos específicos** antes de analizar, lo cual es crítico porque:

**Problemas de los promedios generales:**
1. **Ocultan variación**: Un promedio puede esconder comportamientos opuestos
2. **Paradoja de Simpson**: La tendencia general puede invertirse en cada segmento
3. **Impiden acción**: "Mejorar conversión" es muy vago; "mejorar conversión en mobile US" es accionable

**Dimensiones comunes de segmentación:**
- **Geografía**: País, región, ciudad
- **Canal**: Orgánico, paid, referral, directo
- **Dispositivo**: Desktop, mobile, tablet
- **Tipo de cliente**: Nuevo vs recurrente, B2B vs B2C
- **Cohorte**: Fecha de adquisición

## 🇺🇸 El Script en Inglés (Para la entrevista)

> *"Segmentation is fundamental to honest data analysis. The reason is simple: aggregates lie. A single average across all users can completely mask what's actually happening in your business. I've seen this play out countless times—a company sees stable overall conversion and assumes everything is fine, only to discover that desktop conversion is soaring while mobile is tanking, and the two cancel out in the aggregate. Or a marketing team celebrates a campaign's overall success, but segmentation reveals it only worked for one demographic while alienating another. There's even a name for this phenomenon: Simpson's Paradox, where a trend appears in aggregate data but reverses or disappears when you break it down by subgroups. Beyond avoiding false conclusions, segmentation is what makes analysis actionable. 'Our conversion rate dropped 20%' is a problem statement. 'Our conversion rate dropped 40% for mobile users in Latin America after the checkout redesign' is a diagnosis that points to a fix. In my work, I automatically segment by at least four dimensions: geography, channel, device, and customer type. It's become muscle memory—never trust the topline number without seeing what's underneath."*

## 🔑 Vocabulario Clave

* **Aggregates lie:** Los agregados mienten
* **Mask:** Ocultar / enmascarar
* **Simpson's Paradox:** Paradoja de Simpson
* **Subgroups:** Subgrupos
* **Topline number:** Número general / principal
