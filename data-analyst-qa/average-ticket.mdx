## 💻 La Respuesta Técnica

El **ticket promedio** (Average Order Value - AOV) representa el **monto promedio gastado por transacción**:

**Fórmula:**
```
Ticket Promedio = Ingresos totales / Número de transacciones
```

**Utilidad de negocio:**
1. **Benchmarking**: Comparar performance entre periodos, canales, segmentos
2. **Estrategia de pricing**: Evaluar impacto de descuentos, bundles, upsells
3. **Segmentación de clientes**: Identificar high-value vs low-value customers
4. **Proyecciones**: Estimar revenue esperado basado en tráfico × conversión × ticket

**Ejemplo:**
- Ingresos mensuales: $500,000
- Transacciones: 10,000
- Ticket promedio: $50 por compra

## 🇺🇸 El Script en Inglés (Para la entrevista)

> *"Average ticket, or Average Order Value (AOV), is one of the three core drivers of revenue—alongside traffic and conversion rate. It tells you how much customers spend per transaction on average. The formula is straightforward: total revenue divided by number of transactions. But the strategic value is in what you can do with it. First, it's a diagnostic tool: if revenue is up but AOV is down, you know you're driving growth through discounts or lower-value products, which may not be sustainable. Second, it's a lever for growth: increasing AOV by 10% has the same revenue impact as getting 10% more customers, but often at lower acquisition cost. I've worked on initiatives like product bundling, free shipping thresholds, and cross-sell recommendations specifically to move this metric. Third, it's essential for segmentation: customers with consistently high AOV are your best candidates for loyalty programs or VIP treatment. In my analysis, I always slice AOV by channel, product category, and customer cohort—because a single average often hides massive variation. For instance, mobile might have a $30 AOV while desktop is $70, which has huge implications for marketing spend and UX priorities."*
