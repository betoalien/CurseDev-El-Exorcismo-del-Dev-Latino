## 🛑 El Error del Junior

"Lo pongo arriba"—muy básico. No entiende principios de diseño visual ni jerarquía.

## 🧠 El Enfoque Curse Dev (Contra-pregunta)

* **Tú:** *"Highlighting insights is about visual design and information hierarchy. Would you like me to share specific techniques I use?"*

## 💻 La Respuesta Técnica

**Técnicas para destacar el insight principal:**

**1. Posición estratégica:**
- Zona superior izquierda (patrón de lectura en F)
- Above the fold: visible sin scroll

**2. Jerarquía visual:**
- Tamaño más grande que otros elementos
- Peso visual (bold, colores contrastantes)

**3. Contexto explícito:**
- Títulos que comunican el insight, no solo el tema
  - ❌ "Ventas por región"
  - ✅ "Norte supera target 150%, Sur requiere atención"
- Annotations que explican anomalías o cambios

**4. Color estratégico:**
- Color único para resaltar (el resto en gris/neutro)
- Semáforo: verde (bien), rojo (problema), amarillo (atención)

**5. KPI cards destacadas:**
- Números grandes con indicador de tendencia
- Comparativo vs target o periodo anterior

## 🇺🇸 El Script en Inglés (Para la entrevista)

> *"Highlighting the main insight is about intentional design—I want the stakeholder to grasp the key message within seconds, not hunt for it. First, position: I place the most critical insight top-left, following the natural F-pattern of how people scan dashboards. It should be 'above the fold'—visible without scrolling. Second, visual hierarchy: the primary insight gets the most screen real estate—larger font, bolder treatment, more white space around it. Third, I write insight-driven titles, not just labels. Instead of 'Sales by Region,' I'll write 'North Region Exceeds Target by 50%—South Needs Attention.' That tells the story immediately. Fourth, I use color strategically—if everything is colorful, nothing stands out. So I'll render supporting metrics in neutral gray and use a single accent color for the key insight. Fifth, I add annotations for context: 'Spike due to holiday campaign' or 'Drop correlates with site outage on 3/15.' These small touches transform a dashboard from 'here's data' to 'here's what you need to know.' I've had executives tell me my dashboards 'read themselves'—that's the goal."*

## 🔑 Vocabulario Clave

* **Visual hierarchy:** Jerarquía visual
* **Above the fold:** Por encima del pliegue (visible sin scroll)
* **Insight-driven:** Guiado por insights
* **Accent color:** Color de acento
* **Annotations:** Anotaciones
