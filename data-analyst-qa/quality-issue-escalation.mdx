## 🛑 El Error del Junior

"Lo arreglo y ya"—ingenuo. No comunica el problema, no mide impacto, no previene que vuelva a pasar.

## 🧠 El Enfoque Curse Dev (Contra-pregunta)

* **Tú:** *"Data quality issues require both immediate triage and long-term prevention. Would it help if I walked through my incident response framework?"*

## 💻 La Respuesta Técnica

**Proceso de respuesta a problemas de calidad:**

**1. Documentar el hallazgo:**
- ¿Qué métrica está afectada?
- ¿Cuál es el impacto cuantitativo? (ej. "revenue subreportado en 15%")
- ¿Desde cuándo existe el problema?
- ¿Qué segmentos o regiones afecta?

**2. Medir el impacto:**
```sql
-- Cuantificar diferencia
SELECT
    'Before' as period, COUNT(*) as cnt, SUM(amount) as total
FROM orders WHERE order_date < '2025-03-01'
UNION ALL
SELECT
    'After' as period, COUNT(*) as cnt, SUM(amount) as total
FROM orders WHERE order_date >= '2025-03-01';
-- ¿Hay drop inexplicable?
```

**3. Comunicar a stakeholders:**
- Notificar inmediatamente a afectados (ej. equipo de reporting, leadership)
- Proveer contexto: qué pasó, qué se está haciendo, timeline estimado
- Recomendar acciones: ¿posponer decisiones basadas en la métrica?

**4. Proponer solución temporal:**
- ¿Se puede calcular la métrica de forma alternativa?
- ¿Hay datos históricos para estimar lo faltante?
- ¿Se puede excluir el periodo afectado del reporte?

**5. Corregir de fondo:**
- Identificar causa raíz (bug de ETL, cambio de schema, error humano)
- Trabajar con ingeniería para fix permanente
- Agregar tests de calidad para prevenir recurrencia

## 🇺🇸 El Script en Inglés (Para la entrevista)

> *"When I discover a data quality issue affecting a key metric, I treat it like a production incident—because it is. My response follows a structured process. First, I document the finding: which metric is impacted, what's the quantitative impact (like 'revenue is underreported by 20%'), when did it start, and what segments are affected. This gives me scope. Second, I quantify the impact with SQL—comparing affected periods to baselines, isolating which partitions are corrupted. Third, I communicate immediately to stakeholders who rely on that metric. I've learned that bad news delivered early is better than surprises later. I tell them what I know, what I'm doing, and when I expect to have answers. Fourth, if possible, I propose a workaround—can we calculate the metric from a different source? Can we exclude the corrupted period and show a partial report? Fifth, I work with engineering to identify and fix the root cause—whether it's a broken ETL job, a schema change, or a bug in transformation logic. Finally, I advocate for preventive measures: adding data quality tests to the pipeline, setting up alerts for anomalies, or improving documentation. I once found that a 'simple' filter change was excluding an entire region's revenue. By catching it early, quantifying the $2M impact, and escalating quickly, we fixed the pipeline within hours and avoided a misleading board report. The key is to be proactive, not reactive."*

## 🔑 Vocabulario Clave

* **Production incident:** Incidente de producción
* **Quantitative impact:** Impacto cuantitativo
* **Stakeholders:** Partes interesadas
* **Workaround:** Solución temporal / alternativa
* **Root cause:** Causa raíz
