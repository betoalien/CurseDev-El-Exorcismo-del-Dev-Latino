## 🛑 El Error del Junior

"Para ver dónde se pierden usuarios"—correcto pero no explica la metodología ni cómo priorizar mejoras.

## 🧠 El Enfoque Curse Dev (Contra-pregunta)

* **Tú:** *"Funnel analysis is one of the most actionable frameworks I use. Would it help if I walked through how to identify and prioritize drop-off points?"*

## 💻 La Respuesta Técnica

El **funnel de conversión** mapea las **etapas secuenciales** que un usuario recorre hasta completar una meta:

**Propósito:**
1. **Visualizar el journey**: Del primer touchpoint a la conversión
2. **Identificar fricción**: Dónde y cuántos usuarios abandonan
3. **Priorizar mejoras**: Enfocarse en etapas con mayor impacto potencial
4. **Medir progreso**: Tracking de mejoras de UX a través del tiempo

**Métricas clave:**
- **Drop-off rate**: Porcentaje que abandona en cada etapa
- **Stage-to-stage conversion**: Eficiencia entre etapas consecutivas

**Ejemplo (E-commerce):**
```
1. Visita al sitio: 10,000 usuarios
2. View producto: 4,000 (40% conversión)
3. Add al carrito: 1,000 (25% del paso anterior)
4. Checkout iniciado: 500 (50%)
5. Compra completada: 250 (50%)
```

## 🇺🇸 El Script en Inglés (Para la entrevista)

> *"Funnel analysis is one of the most actionable frameworks in an analyst's toolkit. It breaks down the customer journey into sequential stages—from first awareness to final conversion—and quantifies how many users progress or drop off at each step. The power of this approach is that it turns a vague problem like 'we need more sales' into specific, prioritized opportunities. For example, if you see that 60% of users drop off between 'add to cart' and 'checkout started,' you've identified a high-impact area to investigate—maybe the checkout button is hard to find, or shipping costs are revealed too late. I always calculate two metrics: the step-to-step conversion rate, which shows efficiency between consecutive stages, and the overall funnel conversion, which is end-to-end performance. Then I work with product and UX teams to run experiments—simplifying forms, adding trust signals, optimizing page load times—and measure the lift. The key insight is that small improvements in early stages compound: a 10% lift at the top of the funnel flows through to the bottom. I've used funnel analysis for e-commerce purchases, SaaS onboarding, loan applications, and even hiring pipelines—it's universally applicable wherever there's a multi-step process."*

## 🔑 Vocabulario Clave

* **Actionable frameworks:** Marcos accionables
* **Sequential stages:** Etapas secuenciales
* **Drop-off rate:** Tasa de abandono
* **Step-to-step conversion:** Conversión paso a paso
* **Lift:** Mejora / incremento
